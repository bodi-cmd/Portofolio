HealthyC (or HLTC)

The tobacco epidemic is one of the biggest public health threats the world has ever faced, killing more than 8 million people a year around the world. More than 7 million of those deaths are the result of direct tobacco use while around 1.2 million are the result of non-smokers being exposed to second-hand smoke.

In order to help those people, I decided to build this product. The HealthyC is a smart cigarette pack, which will help you quit smoking. The main principle of this device is dosing the ammount of cigarettes you can take over time. After each cigarette, you have to wait an amount of time, which will increase slowly by each cigarette you take.

The most complex component of this project was the case. I tried to make it portable, but still give it a big battery, which can last weeks, and decent storage for cigarettes (around 20 pcs.). We will talk about the next components later.

Casing

As I said, the case was very hard to design. I modelled it in Creo Parametric, and then I 3D printed it with a ZORTRAX M200 printer.



Powering

For power I used a Li-Ion Battery salvaged from an old smartphone. I choosed it because it's lightweight, thin and has a lot of charge (1800mAh). The nominal voltage of this battery is 3.7V, so I had to use a step-up boost converter to raise the voltage to 5V. For charging the battery, I needed a step-down converter. Luckly, I found this USB power bank circuit, which have both step-up and step-down converters for cheap. Now we can use the pack as a portable power bank too !



The Motherboard

The brain of this project is an Arduino Nano. It is connected to gyroscope for detecting user's steps, and also connected to a RTC (Real Time Clock) for knowing the exact time, even if the device was powered off. The pack is being unlocked by a 9g Servomotor with the help of IR Intreruping sensor, which sees when a cigarette is being taken and tells the 'brain' to lock it again. All the information is being shown on an OLED Display, with the I2C communication for easy conencting. I made all the components detachables, so I can reuse them for other projects.



Assembling

Luckly, the case was perfect by the first try, so the assembling was easy.
